Local Food in Surabaya

Rawon

Rawon is a rich, flavorful beef soup that is known for its distinct black color, which comes from the use of keluak (fermented black nuts). The soup has an earthy and slightly nutty taste, balanced with a blend of spices.

The beef is slow-cooked until tender, absorbing the deep, savory flavors of the broth. Rawon is typically served with white rice, sambal, boiled eggs, and kerupuk. This hearty dish is a favorite comfort food in Surabaya.

Lontong Balap

Lontong Balap is a beloved street food made from lontong (rice cakes), bean sprouts, fried tofu, and lentho (fried mashed black-eyed peas). These ingredients are served in a light and savory broth, often garnished with fried shallots.

It is usually eaten with sate kerang (clam satay), adding a unique texture and flavor contrast. The dish has a comforting, mild taste, and is commonly enjoyed by locals as a midday meal or snack.

Semanggi

Semanggi is a traditional dish that uses boiled semanggi (clover leaves) as the main ingredient, mixed with bean sprouts and sometimes other vegetables. It is served with a sweet and savory peanut sauce, which is made from ground peanuts, tamarind, and gula jawa (palm sugar).

The dish is often accompanied by kerupuk to add crunch. Semanggi is unique to Surabaya and is typically sold by local street vendors, often carried in a traditional woven basket.

Rujak Cingur

Rujak Cingur is a traditional salad that combines fruits, vegetables, and cingur (boiled cow's snout). The mixture typically includes fruits like young mango, pineapple, and cucumber, along with vegetables such as bean sprouts, water spinach, and tofu.

The entire dish is tossed in a thick, spicy sauce made from petis (fermented shrimp paste), peanuts, and chili, giving it a bold, complex flavor. The cingur adds a unique chewy texture to this Surabaya specialty, making it one of the most distinctive dishes in the region.
