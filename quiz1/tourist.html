Top Tourist Places in Surabaya

Tugu Pahlawan

Tugu Pahlawan is one of the most iconic landmarks in Surabaya, symbolizing the bravery and patriotism of Indonesian heroes. This towering 41-meter obelisk is dedicated to the fallen heroes of the Battle of Surabaya in 1945, a pivotal event in Indonesia's struggle for independence.

Surrounding the monument is the Museum 10 Nopember, which houses historical artifacts, documents, and multimedia exhibits related to the battle and the broader Indonesian fight for independence. Tugu Pahlawan is a place of pride for the people of Surabaya, reminding visitors of the city's significant role in the country's history.

Jembatan Suramadu

Jembatan Suramadu is a remarkable feat of modern engineering, stretching 5.4 kilometers across the Madura Strait to connect Surabaya on the island of Java with Bangkalan on the island of Madura. It is the longest bridge in Indonesia and a vital link that boosts economic development between the two regions.

Opened in 2009, Jembatan Suramadu has become a symbol of Surabaya's modernization and a popular spot for visitors, offering breathtaking views, especially during sunrise or sunset. The bridge not only serves as a crucial transportation link but also represents the city's progress and connection to other regions.

Taman Bungkul

Taman Bungkul is one of Surabaya's most popular public parks, located in the heart of the city. It's a vibrant and multifunctional green space that offers both relaxation and recreation for residents and tourists. The park is well-equipped with jogging tracks, a skateboard park, a playground for children, and a stage for cultural performances.

What makes Taman Bungkul unique is its historical significance, as it is named after a revered religious figure, Sunan Bungkul, whose grave is located nearby. It's a perfect spot for families, sports enthusiasts, or those who want to relax and enjoy the greenery.

Kebun Binatang Surabaya

Kebun Binatang Surabaya is one of the oldest and largest zoos in Southeast Asia founded in 1916. The zoo is home to more than 230 different animal species consisting of more than 2179 animals. It offers visitors the chance to see endangered species like the Komodo dragon and the Sumatran tiger.

The zoo plays a significant role in wildlife conservation and education, making it a popular destination for families and school groups. In addition to animal exhibits, the zoo provides interactive experiences, such as feeding sessions and educational programs.
